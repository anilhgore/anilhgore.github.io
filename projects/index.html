Research Projects

Funded and ongoing research projects in analytical chemistry, sustainable nanotechnology, and environmental remediation.

Active
2024

Gas Chromatography for Emerging Environmental Pollutants

CSR-funded project (Rs. 17,04,400/-) focusing on Gas Chromatography as a key tool for analysis of emerging environmental pollutants, studying their impact on climate change, and creating social awareness. Funded by Neterwala Group (UTPL, OFI, UDT).

By: Anil H. Gore
Active
2023

Carbonaceous Eco-Hydrogel for Environmental Remediation

Development of agro-waste valorized carbonaceous eco-hydrogels following circular economy and zero waste principles for removal of emerging contaminants like doxorubicin, tetracycline, and heavy metal ions from water and wastewater.

By: Anil H. Gore , Pooja V. Deore , Omkar S. Nille
Active
2023

Analysis of Nitrosamine Impurities in Pharmaceuticals

Development of ultrasensitive LC-MS/MS and GC-MS methods for quantitation of N-nitrosamine genotoxic impurities in pharmaceutical drug substances. Addressing regulatory compliance through advanced analytical method development and validation.

By: Anil H. Gore , Anant D. Yadav
Active
2021

Micro/Nanoplastics Separation & Analysis

Comprehensive extraction and analysis of microplastics and nanoplastics from various environmental and food samples including rice, soil, water, and personal care products. Developing green miniaturized analytical methodologies and assessing ecological and human health risks of microplastic contamination.

By: Anil H. Gore , Pinal S. Bhavsar , Yasuhito Shimada
Active
2020

Biopolymer Nanocomposites for Sustainable UV Protective Packaging

Design and development of biopolymer-based nanocomposite materials for sustainable food packaging with UV shielding properties. Using waste-derived carbon dots and nanofillers to create transparent, flexible, and environmentally friendly packaging films.

By: Anil H. Gore
Active
2018

Biomass & Waste Valorization for Multifaceted Applications

Sustainable conversion of waste biomass (tea residue, sugarcane molasses, corn comb, spent carbon) into functional carbonaceous nanomaterials for applications in water treatment, agriculture, sensing, and energy storage following green chemistry principles.

By: Anil H. Gore , Chandrashekhar S. Patil , Omkar S. Nille , Govind B. Kolekar
Completed
2016

Photophysical Study of Electrospun Nanofibers with Nanomaterials for Environmental Remediation

DST-SERB funded Young Scientist project (Rs. 32,67,000/-) on photophysical properties of electrospun nanofiber membranes incorporated with carbonaceous nanomaterials and quantum dots for environmental remediation applications including water purification and pollutant detection.

By: Anil H. Gore , Govind B. Kolekar
Completed
2011

Quantum Dot & Carbon Dot Based Nanosensors

Development of fluorescent and colorimetric nanosensors using quantum dots, carbon dots, and metal nanoparticles for selective detection of heavy metal ions, pharmaceuticals, pesticides, and biomolecules in environmental and biomedical samples.

By: Anil H. Gore , Govind B. Kolekar , Prashant V. Anbhule
